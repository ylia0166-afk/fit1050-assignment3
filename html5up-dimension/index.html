About Us

Social of Arts Club is a fictional student community created for students who enjoy creativity, friendship, and campus life. The club provides a welcoming space where members can meet new people, share ideas, join creative activities, and take part in social events outside the classroom. Through workshops, art-inspired gatherings, student meetups, networking sessions, and major club events, Social of Arts Club encourages students to build confidence, explore their interests, and feel connected with others. The club is designed for both beginners and experienced creatives, so students do not need professional art skills to join. Whether someone wants to relax after study, make friends, attend fun events, or express their creative side, Social of Arts Club offers an inclusive and energetic environment where student life becomes more meaningful, social, and memorable..

Social Events

Campus Picnic & Games

Campus Picnic & Games is one of our most relaxed and welcoming social events. It gives students a chance to step away from study, enjoy the outdoors, and spend time with others in a friendly open space. Members can bring snacks, sit together on the grass, join small group games, or simply chat with new people in a casual environment. The event is designed to be easy to join, even for students who may feel nervous about attending club activities for the first time. Through light games, shared food, creative conversations, and team activities, students can build friendships naturally without pressure. This event is especially attractive because it combines relaxation, social connection, and fun campus life in one experience. Whether students want to meet new friends, enjoy a sunny afternoon, or feel more connected to the club community, Campus Picnic & Games provides a warm and enjoyable way to take part.

Creative Meetup Night

Creative Meetup Night is a relaxed evening event designed for students who want to meet new people, share ideas, and enjoy a creative social atmosphere. During the event, members can take part in casual conversations, small creative activities, group discussions, and icebreaker games. It is not only a place for students with strong artistic skills, but also for beginners who simply enjoy creativity and want to connect with others. The event gives students a comfortable space to talk about art, design, music, photography, campus life, or personal interests. With friendly lighting, music, snacks, and open conversation, Creative Meetup Night helps members feel included and confident. It is especially suitable for new students because it makes meeting people easier and less awkward. By joining this event, students can build friendships, discover shared interests, and become more involved in the club community.

Gallery Walk / Art Exhibition Visit

Art Exhibition / Gallery Walk is a cultural and creative activity where members visit an art exhibition together and explore different forms of visual expression. During the event, students can walk through the gallery, observe artworks, discuss their personal interpretations, and share ideas with other members in a relaxed environment. This activity is not only for students with strong art knowledge, but also for anyone who is curious about creativity and wants to experience art in a social way. By joining the exhibition visit, members can discover new artistic styles, gain inspiration, and learn how different artists communicate emotions, stories, and social ideas through their work. The event also gives students a chance to connect through meaningful conversations rather than only casual chatting. It is attractive because it combines learning, creativity, friendship, and cultural experience in one activity. For new members, Art Exhibition / Gallery Walk is a comfortable way to meet others while enjoying a calm and inspiring atmosphere.

Creative Market Day

Creative Market Day is a lively event where members can display, share, and celebrate their creative work in a friendly student community. During the event, students can set up small stalls to showcase handmade crafts, drawings, prints, postcards, jewellery, photography, stickers, or other personal creative projects. It gives members a chance to express their ideas, receive encouragement from others, and feel proud of their work. This event is attractive because it combines creativity, social interaction, and a relaxed market atmosphere. Students do not need to be professional artists to join; they can simply bring something they enjoy making or come as visitors to support others. Creative Market Day also helps members meet people with similar interests, exchange inspiration, and discover different styles of student creativity. It creates a warm and energetic space where art becomes more social, accessible, and enjoyable.

Our Arts Guide

Our Academic Arts Guide is designed to help students understand how creativity can support academic growth. The club believes that art is not only a form of expression, but also a way of thinking, questioning, and communicating ideas. Through student-led resources, study discussions, creative workshops, and peer support activities, we encourage members to explore the connection between arts, culture, society, and personal development. This initiative aims to make academic learning more approachable by sharing useful guidance, practical study tips, and creative perspectives from students for students. Whether members are planning their studies, developing new interests, or looking for inspiration beyond the classroom, our arts guide provides a supportive space where learning becomes more meaningful, creative, and connected to real student experiences.

More Information

Journals & Creative Arts

Our creative journal is a student-led publication created to celebrate imagination, self-expression, and original ideas. It provides a welcoming space for students to share their writing, artwork, photography, design pieces, poetry, short stories, and other creative projects. The journal is not about perfection. It is about confidence, creativity, and giving every student a chance to express their voice.

We welcome submissions from students of all creative backgrounds. Members can submit written pieces such as poems, short stories, reflections, or creative essays. They can also submit visual works such as drawings, digital art, photography, paintings, posters, or mixed-media pieces. Each submission should show personal expression, creative thinking, or a meaningful story behind the work.

This project aims to help students feel proud of their creative identity and connect with others through shared artistic interests. Selected works may be featured in our online journal, social media showcase, or club exhibition events. Whether students are experienced creators or just beginning to explore art, the journal offers a supportive platform to share ideas, gain confidence, and become part of a creative community.
